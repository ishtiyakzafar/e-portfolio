About Myself

My journey of discovery started when I started reaching out to the communities through the various community activities. I was volunteering with Community Development Council (NorthWest) and was also in the Executive Committee of Zone 9 Residents' Committee. My Saturday afternoons were dedicated to RDA (Riding for the Disabled Association) where I had the golden opportunity to work with disabled children, teaching them to ride horses / ponies. On an ad-hoc basis, I was volunteering with the Rotary Club (Singapore West) as well as the Lions' Club. On top of these, I made sure I was there for family and friends as I ensured I had fulfilment from every commitment.


Soon I anchored myself with DAS Association and here I am, engaged in a very rewarding career, helping dyslexic learners achieve, and also having the opportunity to pursue higher education - Master of Arts (Instructional Design and Technology). I witness myself growing and developing as an individual as well as a team with the Association and am thankful for that.

My Professional Philosophy

My teaching philosophy is rooted in a constructivists approach in that learners must be actively involved in the process of constructing their own meaning and knowledge.


My mission as a teacher is to understand my students and their social, emotional, and intellectual needs so I can inspire them to develop lifelong learning and critical thinking skills. I encourage these skills by teaching through a rigorous and wide range of reading, writing, vocabulary, authentic discussion, cooperative, collaborative, and digital activities that have meaningful connections to the English Language as well the fundamental phonics concepts. I promote equity by placing a high value on differentiated instruction because I appreciate that learners are individuals, all with unique backgrounds and diverse levels of prior knowledge as I do not at all believe that one size fits all.

Me now...

The Covid-19 pandemic has taught me to be more aware of the community I live in. It made me think of how the pandemic has affected not just myself but the people who were having it hard even before the pandemic.


This prompted me to reach out to foreign workers in Singapore through the It's Raining Raincoats initiative - where volunteers basically teach foreign workers to speak in English and develop their speaking skills.


I hope to be able to do this for as long as I can!
